SPACE

  • Space is the vast, three-dimensional expanse that contains all matter and energy, including planets, stars, galaxies, and other celestial bodies. It is often thought of as being empty, but in reality it is filled with a variety of particles and radiation.
  • Stephen Hawking was a theoretical physicist and cosmologist who made significant contributions to our understanding of the universe. He was one of the most renowned scientists of his time.
  • He proposed that, in accordance with the laws of quantum mechanics, black holes emit radiation, now known as Hawking radiation. This radiation causes black holes to lose mass and energy, and eventually to evaporate.
  • He also proposed that the universe began with a singularity, a point of infinite density and temperature, which he called the "big bang."
  • He was diagnosed with a rare form of motor neuron disease (also called ALS) at the age of 21 and was given two years to live. Despite this, he went on to live for more than 50 years and published several books, including the best-selling "A Brief History of Time."
  • He passed away on March 14th 2018. -Hawking has been featured in several documentaries and films, books, and other media. His work and life story have inspired people around the world and continues to influence the scientific community.
